# L'Hopital's Rule

## Case 1: the $0/0$ indeterminate form

Let $f, g$ be differentiable on the interval $(a,b)$. Assume that

$$
\begin{equation*}
f(x) = o(1),\quad g(x)=o(1),\quad x\to a,\quad g'(x)\ne 0.
\end{equation*}
$$

If
$$
\begin{equation*}
\lim_{x\to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} = A \in \mathbb{R}\cup\{\pm\infty\},
\end{equation*}
$$

then
$$
\begin{equation*}
\lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = A.
\end{equation*}
$$



## Case 2: the $\infty/\infty$ indeterminate form

Assume that $g(x)\to\infty$ as $x\to a$, and $g'(x)\neq 0$. If
$$
\begin{equation*}
\lim_{x\to a} \frac{f'(x)}{g'(x)} = A \in \mathbb{R}\cup\{\pm\infty\},
\end{equation*}
$$

then
$$
\begin{equation*}
\lim_{x\to a} \frac{f(x)}{g(x)} = A.
\end{equation*}
$$
